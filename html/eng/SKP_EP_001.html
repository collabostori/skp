Mutual Growth

T Academy

T Academy shares SK Planet’s accumulated ICT-related practical experience and know-how with our business partners, elementary and secondary school students, college students, and the general public. By operating various educational programs, we are seeking opportunities to create social value through identifying and developing talent with software skills that can contribute to the growth of the ICT ecosystem. T academy’s education facilities are located inside Seoul National University’s campus and all classrooms are provided with computers such as MacBook for training in addition to various devices needed for application development and testing. Students are also allowed access to the IT library which carries the latest mobile-related publications.

Talk ON
Seminar

This is an interactive seminar held twice a month for practical skills improvement and to offer career advice for prospective developers (such as university students and job seekers) who are preparing to build their career in the ICT field. Direct and practical education is provided by discussing the latest technology fields such as big data, chatbots, artificial intelligence, etc. Talk ON seminars are held at the T academy education facilities located inside the Seoul Gwanak-gu campus. After completion of the seminar, students can have access to the courses online via T academy’s website.

Smarteen
App Challenge

The Smarteen App. Challenge, co-hosted by SK Planet and the Ministry of SMEs and Startups, is the largest domestic IT competition program for high school students in Korea for developing apps. Application planning and development specialized education, practical mentoring, etc. are provided to help students directly materialize their creative ideas and winners are selected through a five-month production and evaluation process. Since its first competition held in 2011, it has been identifying competent prospective developers each year.

Smarteen
Software
Academy

Smarteen Software Academy is an afterschool outreach SW education program that is held directly for primary school students from low-income groups, multicultural families, and insular areas that are prone to exclusion. With its objectives aligned with mandatory SW coding education for primary school students, it provides education opportunities for students from vulnerable social groups to build their SW capabilities. The Smarteen SW Academy operates a professional education program that is co-hosted by Software Korea and a volunteer group consisting of computer science professors and graduate school students nationwide.
